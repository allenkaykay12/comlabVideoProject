About the Story

Re/Write

The film opens with a montage of students at NYU Abu Dhabi responding to the question "What if?", showcasing different emotions and reflections. The story then introduces two characters: Sofia, an Economics major preparing for an upcoming exam, and Camila, an Engineering major deeply focused on her Computer-Aided Design (CAD) project. Sofia, feeling tired and restless, encounters a Creative Writing Workshop poster several times around campus. Initially, she ignores it, but after repeated exposure, she decides to attend the workshop, reigniting her passion for storytelling. Meanwhile, Camila, while working on her engineering assignments, begins to grow curious about education and social change. She eventually decides to volunteer as a mentor for first-year students, stepping beyond her technical field.

After both stories are presented, the audience reaches a decision point where two paths are possible. In the first path, Switch Paths, Sofia changes her major to Creative Writing, and Camila double-majors in Engineering and Social Research and Public Policy (SRPP). In the second path, Stay the Same, Sofia remains an Economics major but continues writing as a hobby, while Camila remains in Engineering and maintains her passion for education through side projects. The film concludes with black screens showing the characters' life updates five years later, depending on the path chosen, emphasizing that both decisions — switching paths or staying in the course — lead to meaningful growth.
